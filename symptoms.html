Symptoms

Persistent worry about everyday events, often disproportionate to the actual situation, making it difficult to relax or focus.

A constant feeling of being “on edge,” unable to sit still, or experiencing a sense of internal tension.

Thoughts that move quickly and uncontrollably, making it difficult to concentrate, sleep, or stay focused on one task.

Trouble focusing or staying mentally present, often described as a “foggy” or scattered mind.

A fast or pounding heartbeat often triggered by worry, stress, or panic, even without physical exertion.

Difficulty breathing or feeling like you can’t take a full breath, commonly associated with panic or high anxiety.

Tightness, stiffness, or aches in muscles—often in the shoulders, neck, and jaw—due to prolonged stress.

Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up feeling unrefreshed due to an overactive or worried mind.

Anxiety can affect digestion, causing nausea, stomach pain, butterflies, or digestive discomfort.

A sense that tasks, responsibilities, or emotions are too much to handle, leading to avoidance or distress.
