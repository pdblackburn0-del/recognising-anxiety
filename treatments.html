Running / Aerobic Exercise

Running activates large muscle groups and increases cardiovascular activity, which helps metabolize excess stress hormones like adrenaline and cortisol that fuel the fight-or-flight state. As your body settles into rhythmic movement, it signals to your brain that the threat has passed, activating the parasympathetic “rest-and-digest” system. Over time, aerobic exercise also increases endorphins and brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which improve mood stability and resilience to stress.
